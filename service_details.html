The Art of Haute Chronometry

Mastering The
Mechanical Pulse

Beyond repair, we practice horological resurrection. Our master watchmakers operate at the intersection of microscopic engineering and ancestral craft, ensuring every movement achieves the absolute peak of chronometric performance.

Deep Dive Into The Atelier

The Architecture of Complications

A grand complication watch is a living machines of immense density. Servicing these calibers requires a unique protocol: complete teardown of the split-seconds chronograph modules, perpetual calendar rockers, or minute repeater striking trains.

Every component is digitally mapped. We isolate wear on column-wheel teeth and re-tension hair-thin springs using traditional hand-tools. Each gear is hand-lubricated with up to six different viscosities of synthetic oil to ensure perfect kinetic harmony.

Split-Second Tuning

Synchronization of rattrapante hands to within 1/100th of a second.

Torque Optimization

Mainspring power-reserve curve flattening for isochronal stability.

Micro-Mechanical Fabrication

When original parts no longer exist for vintage Patek Philippe or Vacheron Constantin calibers, we don’t compromise. We fabricate. Our atelier is equipped with high-precision Bergeon lathes and Schaublin milling machines.

From turning a new balance staff to cutting a custom escape wheel from raw hardened steel, our precision is measured in microns. We maintain an archive of historic metal alloys to ensure every new part matches the original weight and coefficient of expansion.

Bessemer-Grade Steel

Turning components with tolerances of ±0.002mm.

Arbor Polishing

Restoring factory-spec mirror finishes to microscopic pivot points.

The Finishing Atelier

A luxury watch is as much an art piece as a machine. Our finishing specialists restore the invisible beauty of the movement.

Anglage

Hand-mirror polishing of beveled edges. We restore the "angles rentrants" (internal angles) using gentian wood and diamond paste for a distortion-free shine.

Perlage & Côtes

Re-applying the emotional circular graining and Côtes de Genève (Geneva Stripes) to bridge plates, ensuring the light play is identical to the day it left Switzerland.

Heat-Bluing

Restoring the deep royal blue of oxidized steel screws and hands. Every component is fire-blued individually to achieve a uniform, luminous indigo hue.

Case Lapping & Geometric Preservation

Standard polishing is destructive. It rounds off the sharp edges and thins the lugs of a precious metal case. Our atelier utilizes cold laser welding combined with precision lapping machines to restore factory-spec geometry without removing original metal volume.

We separately mask off satin-brushed planes and mirror-polished chamfers. This ensures that the distinct, crisp lines defined by the original manufacture are perfectly preserved, returning the watch to its "new-old-stock" appearance.

Cold Laser Fill

Adding metal back into deep gouges at a molecular level.

Facet Masking

Maintaining clear boundaries between contrasting finishes.

Dial Preservation & Luminescent Signatures

The dial is the face of the timepiece and the most sensitive component. We specialize in the stabilization of vintage tropical dials and the restoration of missing pad-printed signatures using authentic historic fonts and lead-free inks.

Our "Lume Lab" precisely color-matches Super-LumiNova® or non-active aged tritium substitutes to ensure the nocturnal signature of your watch matches its daytime age and provenance perfectly.

Pad Printing

Restoring microscopic logotypes to factory precision.

Patina Stabilization

Protecting original finishes from further environmental degradation.

The 600-Hour Protocol

No watch leaves the atelier without surviving our rigorous final assessment cycle.

Pressure Certification

Dry vacuum testing followed by wet pressure cycles up to 300m (30 ATM). We verify the hermetic seal of every crown, pusher, and sapphire seating.

Position Mapping

Final observation over 21 days in six simulated real-wear positions. We chart the amplitude and beat-error until it falls within CHRONOMETER specifications.

Digital Passport

You receive a comprehensive service diary, including macroscopic before/after photos and a digital timegrapher report signed by the lead artisan.
